The Way of the Ronin

A ronin answers to the standard, not to an ego.

The ronin was a warrior without a master — free, but bound by their own code. We named the academy after that idea because the training here isn't about worshipping a style or a coach. It's about the daily, unglamorous work of showing up, bowing in, and getting a little sharper than yesterday.

We opened Ronin in 2014 in a small first-floor room with four mats and a heavy bag. Today we run four disciplines and forty-plus classes a week, but the rule hasn't changed: leave your rank at the door, help the person next to you, and let the mat teach you the rest.

  • 礼 Respect first — we bow in and we bow out.
  • 忍 Endure — controlled pressure, never reckless.
  • 心 Composure — calm hands, clear head, off the mat too.

The belt path

Rank is earned in years, not weeks.

At Ronin, a promotion means something. Belts are awarded on the mat, in front of your teammates, when the coaches agree the technique — and the temperament — are there. Here is the adult BJJ road ahead of you.

  1. White Start here
  2. Blue ~2 years
  3. Purple ~4 years
  4. Brown ~6 years
  5. Black ~10 years

Kids grade differently. Under-16s follow the grey / yellow / orange / green belt system, with stripes awarded far more often to keep young students motivated and honest about their progress.

No mystery fees. Gradings are included in your membership. When you're ready, you test — we never sell a belt.
