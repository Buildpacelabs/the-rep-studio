The Shastra Way

A dojo, not a drop-in gym. We teach the art before the fight — posture, breath, patience, and then power.

"Shastra" is the Sanskrit word for a disciplined art carried with intent. We named the academy for it because striking and grappling are, at heart, a study — of leverage, of timing, of your own composure under pressure.

Founded in 2015 on a single mat in Koramangala, Shastra has grown into a full training floor without losing the etiquette that makes a dojo work. Beginners are coached, not thrown to the wolves. Competitors are pushed, not coddled. Everyone bows in the same.

The rank path

Earn every stripe

Belts aren't bought at Shastra — they're tested for. Our BJJ syllabus follows the IBJJF adult grades, and each rank is awarded on mat time, technical exams and how you carry yourself with training partners.

Most students take their first grading around the four-month mark. Your coach will tell you when you're ready — and exactly what to sharpen before you are.
