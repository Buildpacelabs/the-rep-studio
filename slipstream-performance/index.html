01 — The method

Guessing is not a training plan.

Slipstream started in a bare warehouse off Road No. 12 with one stopwatch, a jump mat, and a stubborn belief: if you can measure it, you can move it. Everything here runs on that loop — test, prescribe, coach, retest.

You won't find a wall of mirrors and a random circuit. You'll find a lab floor where every session is written for the athlete standing on it, and where "good workout" is a number, not a feeling.

Measured

Fourteen baseline metrics, retested every six weeks.

Coached

Groups capped at six so form never drifts.

Progressed

Loads and targets adjust to your last session, not a template.

05 — The Slipstream loop

From assessment to a programme that fits.

Four steps, one continuous loop. This is what makes a Slipstream session different from a workout.

  1. 01

    Assess

    A 60-minute baseline: sprint velocity, jump force, strength, engine and a full movement screen. Fourteen data points, zero guesswork.

  2. 02

    Prescribe

    Your coach reads the profile and writes the block — the right programme, the right loads, the right weekly split for where you actually are.

  3. 03

    Coach

    Every rep is watched. Groups of six, live cues, and load adjusted on the floor when the numbers say you have more.

  4. 04

    Retest

    Every six weeks we run the baseline again. You see the deltas, we rewrite the block, and the loop starts over — faster this time.
