Why we exist

Recovery is the workout you're skipping.

The Recovery Room began after our founder tore a hamstring six weeks before a marathon and discovered that Bengaluru had studios for every kind of effort — and almost nowhere calm and clinical to undo the damage.

So we built a room that treats recovery as training: measured, guided, and slightly cold. No loud music, no mirrors, no ego. Just the tools that move you from wrecked to ready — cryo, heat, compression and honest mobility work — run by people who read your body, not a stopwatch.

  • Protocol-led. Every session is guided, not guessed.
  • Clinically minded. Physio-informed, athlete-tested.
  • Genuinely calm. Cool tones, quiet rooms, unhurried staff.

The protocols

Six ways to reset the body.

Mix and match modalities into a session that fits your training load. Our team will build the sequence — you just show up and breathe.

01

Cryotherapy

Three minutes in a −85°C whole-body chamber to blunt inflammation, sharpen focus and speed muscle turnaround.

3 min · post-training

02

Infrared Sauna

Deep radiant heat that loosens tissue, calms the nervous system and preps the body before cold work.

45 min · warm-up or wind-down

03

Cold Plunge

A 4°C immersion with guided breath-work — the original reset, run by a coach who keeps you steady.

2–4 min · guided

04

Compression Therapy

Dynamic pneumatic boots that flush heavy legs, reduce swelling and leave you spring-loaded for the next session.

30 min · legs & hips

05

Mobility & Movement

Physio-led reformer and mobility work to rebuild the range your training keeps taking away.

50 min · coached

06

Recovery Massage

Deep-tissue and percussive therapy from trained hands — targeted where your data and your training say it hurts.

60 min · therapist-led

The method

Warm, reset, restore — in that order.

Recovery works best as a sequence. Here's the room's signature loop, and why it lands the way it does.

  1. 1

    Assess

    A two-minute movement screen tells the coach where you're tight, sore or overloaded today.

  2. 2

    Warm

    Infrared heat opens the tissue and drops your heart rate before any cold or compression work.

  3. 3

    Reset

    Cryo or a cold plunge triggers the vasoconstriction response that clears fatigue and lifts alertness.

  4. 4

    Restore

    Compression and hands-on work close the loop, so you leave lighter than you walked in.
