The house rules

We built the room we always wanted to ride in.

Voltage started with three friends, one dead treadmill, and a stubborn belief that cardio should feel like a night out — not a punishment. So we blacked out the windows, tuned the sound to hit in your chest, and wired every bike to a lighting rig that moves with the music.

No mirrors judging you. No leaderboard shame. Just you, a knob of resistance, and a coach counting you into the drop. Show up as you are — the dark does the rest.

  • Beat-mapped. Every ride is choreographed to a real set, drop for drop.
  • Small floor. 34 bikes, so a coach can actually see you ride.
  • All levels. You control the resistance — the room controls the vibe.
Est. Bengaluru
